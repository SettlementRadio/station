**What this covers** — how the worlds keep order and do justice when no authority is near and no
appeal arrives quickly. Law here is local-but-shared: common compacts everyone honours, enforced
mostly by the people on the spot.

**Building blocks to consider**
- Shared compacts / charters vs. purely local law.
- How justice is actually *done* when courts and help are weeks away.
- Rights and their limits; what every settled person is owed.
- Crime, redress, exile, and second chances.
- The law of the ships and of the between — who rules where no world does.

**The tribute** — the genre's moral seriousness: the just society as a thought experiment, rights and
duties reasoned through rather than assumed.

**What this covers** — religion and meaning-making: faiths old and transformed, faiths newly born among
the worlds, the rites people keep when home is centuries behind and the night is wide. Treated as a
human universal, with seriousness and warmth — never mockery.

**Building blocks to consider**
- Old faiths that survived the diaspora, and how they changed in crossing.
- New faiths born among the worlds — of travel, of light, of the between.
- Rites tied to distance, departure, the dead, and the dark.
- The secular and the spiritual; how believers and non-believers share a world.
- Festivals and holy days (cf. the Lumen Festival — its dated instance lives in `95-events.md`).

**The tribute** — the genre's serious treatment of belief: meaning sought honestly in an indifferent
cosmos, the sacred examined rather than dismissed.

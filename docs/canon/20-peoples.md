**What this covers** — the *peoples*, not individuals (named people are cast in `90-cast.md` and,
later, D10 figures). After centuries apart, humanity has branched: worlds shaped their settlers, and
"human" now spans a spectrum. This file is the world's anthropology of identity.

**Building blocks to consider**
- The major peoples / lineages and the worlds that formed them.
- Bodily and cultural adaptation to different environments (gravity, light, air, isolation).
- How the worlds see one another — kinship, prejudice, fascination, rivalry.
- What "human" has come to mean; where the edges of that word are.
- Migration, mixing, and the people of the ships and the between.

**The tribute** — the anthropological strain of the genre: treating invented peoples with an
ethnographer's seriousness and empathy, never caricature. Tribute to the *tradition*, not to names.

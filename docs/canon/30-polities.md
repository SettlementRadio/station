**What this covers** — the political shape of the world: nations, leagues, compacts, and how (or
whether) worlds weeks apart govern themselves and coordinate. No centre can rule at that distance —
so power here is a *designed* problem, not a given.

**Building blocks to consider**
- The major polities / leagues / compacts and their forms of government.
- How they coordinate across distance — councils by relay, slow parliaments, local viceroys.
- Local autonomy vs. federation; what binds worlds that can't be quickly compelled.
- Legitimacy, succession, and the role the relays (and the station) play in public life.
- Standing tensions and alliances (specific flare-ups are events → `95-events.md`).

## Canon facts

1. No authority can rule quickly across weeks of distance, so power is deliberately decentralised: worlds govern themselves and coordinate through slow councils carried on the relays.
   - **Tags:** governance, polity, autonomy, distance, council
2. Worlds bind themselves by honoured compacts rather than enforced command; legitimacy rests on a consent that cannot be compelled at a gun's speed.
   - **Tags:** governance, compact, law, autonomy, legitimacy
3. The relays — and the station on them — are quietly part of public life: the shared channel through which distant worlds argue, decide, and stay one civilisation.
   - **Tags:** governance, relay, station, connection, community

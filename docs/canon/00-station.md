## The station

- **Name:** **Settlement Radio** — the talking companion station of the settled worlds.
- **Year (in-world):** always `real year + 600`, computed at generation time — never written as
  a fixed number anywhere (a fixed year goes stale as real time moves).
- **Premise:** a continuously broadcasting radio station for the scattered human settlements,
  six centuries on from the real present. It plays music, reads the news of the era, and keeps
  people company across the dark between worlds.
- **Spirit / DNA:** optimism tempered by hard questions; wonder; moral seriousness; warmth. A
  tribute to the golden-age and new-wave authors — their *spirit*, never their characters or IP.
- **Tone:** cozy, intelligent, a little wry. Not dystopian, not camp.

The station drifts in the dark between the lanes, a wheel of modules and antennae turning slowly to keep its face toward the worlds it serves. Inside, the walls are warm with use — wood panels from a dozen forests, metal that rings soft when touched, fabric that has absorbed years of music and talk. The DJs live there in overlapping shifts, never quite a full crew, never quite alone. They cook meals in a small galley, argue about setlists, tend to the hydroponic garden that filters the air and supplies the occasional salad. The station is not a machine. It is a home that happens to broadcast.

The signal carries on frequencies that bend through the relay network, hopping from node to node across the void. A listener on Meridian might hear the same song as one on Cold Harbor, separated by minutes or hours depending on the geometry of the worlds. The station does not acknowledge this lag. It speaks as if to one person in a dim room, which is how most people listen — alone, or in small groups, the voice keeping company with the dark outside.

## Canon facts

1. Humanity lives across many settlements; Earth is distant history, spoken of fondly.
   - **Tags:** settlements, humanity, earth, history, home, diaspora, origins, nostalgia, memory, scattered
2. Travel between worlds takes weeks; radio is the thread that connects them.
   - **Tags:** travel, distance, radio, connection, isolation, loneliness, companionship, communication, journey, separation, comfort
3. The station broadcasts from a drifting relay station that holds station between the worlds —
   never quite anywhere, always between, which is why it can talk to everyone.
   - **Tags:** station, relay, broadcast, isolation, neutrality, between, drift, signal, belonging, place
4. **The word "settlement" is a linguistic fossil.** The worlds are long mature — dense, urban,
   planet-spanning cities — but six centuries of habit keep the old frontier word alive, the way
   people once "dialled" phones long after dials were gone. Calling them "the settlements" is an
   act of remembering being small. The station's name carries that memory.
   - **Tags:** settlements, language, memory, history, naming, fossil, words, habit, frontier, remembering, identity, nostalgia
5. There is an annual event, the **Lumen Festival**, with music and lights — every world kindles
   its lamps at the same shared hour. (Its dated instance lives in the Events timeline,
   `95-events.md`.)
   - **Tags:** festival, lumen, music, lights, celebration, tradition, ritual, synchrony, hope, togetherness, annual, lamps, joy
6. Letters between worlds travel as compressed bursts riding the same relays as the broadcast, so
   a listener's message can take weeks to arrive — the DJ sometimes reads ones sent long ago,
   answering people across a gap of time as well as distance.
   - **Tags:** letters, messages, listeners, distance, time, radio, correspondence, longing, delay, waiting, connection, dedications
7. The station was founded early in Settlement Time, when the relay network first reached
   sufficient density to make continuous broadcast possible. For centuries it has been the voice
   in the dark, outlasting polities, wars, and the names of its own founders.
   - **Tags:** station, founding, history, continuity, endurance, relay, age, memory, institution
8. The DJs are not celebrities — most listeners know only their voices, not their faces or
   histories. They are hired by the Relay Authority from ordinary settlements, trained in the
   station's traditions, and serve tours of five years. Some stay longer. Some never leave.
   - **Tags:** djs, anonymity, voice, relay, authority, peoples, tradition, service, belonging
9. The music library contains recordings from every settled world, plus a core of Earth-origin
   material preserved from the first ships. The DJs are given wide latitude in programming, with
   only two rules: no silence longer than thirty seconds, and the midnight hour belongs to the
   listener — requests, dedications, letters read aloud.
   - **Tags:** music, library, earth, origins, preservation, autonomy, programming, rules, midnight, dedications, requests
10. The station maintains a small observatory dome, mostly unused now — automated instruments
    track the relay network, but the telescope remains manual. DJs sometimes climb up to look at
    the worlds they speak to, reduced to points of light. It is considered good luck to sight your
    home world.
    - **Tags:** observatory, dome, telescope, manual, instrument, relay, worlds, sky, home, luck, ritual
11. Power comes from a compact reactor, but backup systems include hand-cranked generators for the
    emergency beacon — a ritual exercise performed monthly, though the beacon has never once been
    needed. The cranking is done to music, a tradition no one remembers starting.
    - **Tags:** power, reactor, backup, manual, generator, beacon, ritual, tradition, monthly, music, endurance
12. The station's position is not fixed — it drifts in a calculated halo, maintaining rough
    equidistance from the major relay nodes. This means its sky changes slowly over the years,
    worlds drifting in and out of direct line of sight. The DJs keep a wall of photographs,
    hand-labelled, showing how the view has changed.
    - **Tags:** drift, halo, position, relay, sky, change, years, photographs, wall, memory, time

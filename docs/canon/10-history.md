**What this covers** — the long road from Earth-as-distant-origin to the present six centuries on:
the diaspora outward, the ages since, the turning points, the losses and renaissances. History
here is *deep* and rarely a straight line — it gives the DJs and the news a past to reach back into.

**Building blocks to consider**
- The great eras / ages — name them and date them roughly (the in-world year is always real + 600;
  never hardcode a fixed present).
- The leaving of Earth, and how Earth is remembered now (distant, spoken of fondly — cf. canon).
- Expansions, contractions, dark periods, recoveries; what was lost and later refound.
- Founding myths vs. recorded history — and where they disagree.
- What every settled child is taught vs. what's been forgotten.

**The tribute** — the deep-time perspective of the genre: history as long, non-linear, and humbling;
optimism tempered by loss. Reference the strain, never specific works or names.

## Canon facts

1. Humanity left Earth in a long, uneven diaspora across centuries; the leaving is remembered less as one event than as a slow tide outward.
   - **Tags:** history, diaspora, origins, earth, memory
2. The ages since the diaspora run in long swings of expansion and contraction — worlds founded, lost, and sometimes refound generations later.
   - **Tags:** history, era, change, loss, past
3. Earth endures as distant origin, spoken of fondly and visited rarely; what every settled child learns of it is half record, half founding myth.
   - **Tags:** earth, origins, memory, history, home

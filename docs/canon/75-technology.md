**What this covers** — the technological baseline: travel, energy, materials, automation, the body and
medicine. Crucially, this file fixes the **limits** that make the whole premise hold — *why travel
still takes weeks*, *why radio is the thread that connects worlds*. Set the ceiling deliberately.

**Building blocks to consider**
- Travel technology — and the hard reason crossings take weeks (no casual faster-than-light hand-wave
  unless you choose it; the slowness is load-bearing for the story).
- Energy and materials; what's cheap, what's precious.
- Everyday automation and machines; what ordinary life takes for granted.
- Medicine, lifespan, and the body (split into its own file later if it grows).
- The line between the mundane and the marvellous — and the things that remain impossible.

**The tribute** — the hard-SF respect for limits: technology with consequences and constraints, not
magic. The wonder is in the rigour.

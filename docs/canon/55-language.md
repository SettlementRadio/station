**What this covers** — language across the dark: the shared tongue(s), how speech drifts when worlds
are centuries and weeks apart, and the fossil-words that outlive their meanings (the word
"settlement" itself is one — cf. `00-station.md`). Language shapes how this world thinks.

**Building blocks to consider**
- The common tongue(s) and how far they actually carry.
- Dialect drift world by world; where speakers can and can't understand each other.
- Loan-words and fossil-words, and the little stories behind them.
- Naming conventions for people, ships, and worlds.
- What's untranslatable; the relays' role in *slowing* the drift apart.

## Canon facts

1. A common tongue carries across the worlds, but centuries and weeks of distance drift it apart, so neighbours can share a word and not quite a meaning.
   - **Tags:** language, dialect, drift, distance, words
2. The worlds are full of fossil-words like "settlement" — terms that outlived their meanings and survive as small acts of memory.
   - **Tags:** language, words, fossil, memory, naming
3. The relays slow the drift of speech apart: a civilisation that keeps hearing the same broadcasts keeps, roughly, the same tongue.
   - **Tags:** language, relay, broadcast, drift, connection

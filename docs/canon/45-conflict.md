**What this covers** — conflict in a world where travel takes weeks: war is slow, ruinously costly,
and strange; peace is the default the station quietly celebrates. This is **not** a militarist world
— conflict is a hard question examined, never a spectacle staged.

**Building blocks to consider**
- The nature of conflict at distance — why a war is measured in seasons, not days.
- Past wars / standoffs and the scars they left (specific flare-ups are events → `95-events.md`).
- How disputes resolve *without* fast force — blockade, embargo, attrition, mediation.
- Piracy and raiding along the routes; the danger of the between.
- The long peace, and what holds it together.

**The tribute** — the anti-militarist, skeptical strain of new-wave SF: war shown with its true cost,
optimism tempered by hard questions — never glorified.

## Canon facts

1. War at this distance is slow, ruinously costly, and rare; force measured in seasons seldom repays what it destroys, so the worlds mostly keep an uneasy long peace.
   - **Tags:** war, peace, distance, conflict, cost
2. Disputes are settled far more often by blockade, embargo, and patient mediation than by fast force.
   - **Tags:** conflict, blockade, mediation, peace, distance
3. Piracy and raiding shadow the long routes — the chief danger of the between — and the relays double as the worlds' shared early warning.
   - **Tags:** piracy, danger, between, route, conflict

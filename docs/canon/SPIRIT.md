# SPIRIT.md — the world's spirit & tribute (an authoring brief)

> **For the writer who scripts the world.** This is the creative north star for the canon you author
> in this folder: the *idea* behind Settlement Radio, the 20th-century science-fiction tradition it
> honours, the authors we draw inspiration from, and the core ideas that should shape every fact and
> page you write. Read it alongside [`README.md`](README.md) (the *format* rules) and
> [`TAGS.md`](TAGS.md) (the tag palette).
>
> **This file is an internal brief — it is NOT world content.** It carries a non-numeric name on
> purpose, so the seeder skips it (only numeric-prefixed cornerstones are loaded); nothing here ever
> reaches the DJs. That's what lets it name real authors freely — see §0.

---

## 0. The one rule that governs everything — the IP firewall

Settlement Radio is a **tribute, not a derivative.** This brief names real authors and movements as
our *compass*; that is fine **here**, because this document is never broadcast and never seeded. But
the **canon you write — and anything the station airs — must be 100% original.**

**Take the spirit. Leave the stuff.**

| ✅ Take (the spirit) | ❌ Never use (the IP) |
|---|---|
| Moods, themes, questions, the *feel* of a tradition | A real author's, work's, or franchise's **name** |
| The *kind* of idea (e.g. "machine ethics as a moral puzzle") | **Named** characters, ships, planets, species, institutions |
| The emotional register (awe, melancholy, wonder) | **Coined terms** an author invented (e.g. a specific word for FTL radio) |
| The *tradition* of a movement (golden age, new wave) | Plot beats, settings, or **quotable lines** from a real work |

**The litmus test:** if a reader could name the source franchise or author from your fact, you've
crossed the line — rewrite until only the *mood or idea* remains, dressed in original specifics.
When in doubt, invent a new name and a new detail. Originality is the whole point of a tribute.

(Reminder: the station *itself* being AI-generated is a separate, out-of-fiction **disclosure** — a
hard rule handled by a spoken ident, not by the fiction. The *world* is classic human SF — see §2.)

## 1. The big idea (the north star)

Settlement Radio is a **love letter to 20th-century science fiction at its best** — the golden age and
the new wave, the era when SF asked big, humane questions with both **wonder and rigour**. The world
exists to *feel* like the finest of that literature:

- **Mature wonder** — awe handled with intelligence and humility, never childish, never cynical.
- **Optimism tempered by hard questions** — not a utopia, not a dystopia; problems reasoned through
  with hope intact.
- **Moral seriousness** — rights, justice, difference, and meaning treated as questions worth the
  effort, never as set-dressing.
- **Warmth** — the human heart kept at the centre of a vast setting.
- **Cosmic awe** — the universe is large and indifferent, and we meet it with a steady, friendly voice.

The house tone (from `00-station.md`): **cozy, intelligent, a little wry. Not dystopian, not camp.**

---

## 2. "Good old sci-fi" — and explicitly NOT modern-AI futurism

The setting is **classic starfaring SF**: a humanity scattered across many worlds, travel that takes
weeks, radio as the thread across the dark, frontier-grown societies, deep time, and the sublime
cosmos. It is the *future*, but imagined with a **golden-age / new-wave** sensibility.

**Keep the fiction free of contemporary AI anxiety.** Avoid:

- the **singularity**, superintelligence "take-off", or "the AI woke up";
- chatbot / large-language-model / algorithmic-feed flavour;
- uploading-as-salvation, or a digital afterlife;
- surveillance-algorithm dystopia as the world's mood.

If thinking machines appear at all, treat them the **mid-century way** — capable *tools* and the
*ethics puzzles* they raise — not as the story's destiny. (This is already fixed in
`25-other-minds.md`: machine minds are tools, not persons; humanity is, so far as it knows, alone.)
The wonder here is **human** — distances, letters, lamps, the radio, the night sky.

## 3. The authors & the strain we take from each

Our palette of influences. For each: **TAKE** (the transferable spirit) and **AVOID** (the concrete IP
to never touch). Some of these authors are recent or living — cite them here as *influences only*;
**none of their creations may appear in canon**, regardless.

### Golden age — rational wonder, big ideas, optimism

- **Isaac Asimov** — *TAKE:* machine ethics as a calm logic-puzzle; history as a vast, almost
  scientific force; deep-time civilisational sweep. *AVOID:* the Three Laws, "positronic", Foundation /
  Trantor / any named character or polity.
- **Arthur C. Clarke** — *TAKE:* the sublime; the small human before the immense; technology met with
  awe and humility; transcendent optimism. *AVOID:* monoliths, named AIs, specific titles or vessels.
- **Robert A. Heinlein** — *TAKE:* competent, self-reliant frontier people; the *lived-in* texture of
  off-world settlement; civic life beyond Earth. *AVOID:* his coinages, named characters/polities, and
  any one ideological doctrine unless you deliberately choose it.
- **Ray Bradbury** — *TAKE:* lyricism, nostalgia, the elegiac ache of distance and memory; the poetry
  of small human moments. *AVOID:* the Martian-chronicle settings and any named story or character.
- **Cordwainer Smith** — *TAKE:* myth-like deep-future strangeness; a far history that reads like
  legend; humane treatment of the not-quite-human. *AVOID:* the Instrumentality, the "underpeople"
  term, named figures.
- **Olaf Stapledon / H. G. Wells** — *TAKE:* civilisational and cosmic deep time; humbling scale;
  the evolutionary long view. *AVOID:* their specific future-histories and titles.

### New wave — literary, social, inward, sceptical

- **Ursula K. Le Guin** — *TAKE (a cornerstone influence):* the **anthropology of invented peoples**
  taken seriously and with empathy; the **ambiguous utopia** (no easy paradise); **communication across
  distance** as a moral and emotional theme; difference treated with dignity. *AVOID:* the Ekumen, the
  Hainish worlds, "ansible", Gethen, any character name.
- **Stanisław Lem** — *TAKE:* epistemic humility — the genuinely alien that stays *unknowable*; dry
  philosophical wit. *AVOID:* Solaris and any named work.
- **The Strugatsky brothers** — *TAKE:* the mundane brushing against the marvellous; bureaucracy and
  wonder side by side; moral murk. *AVOID:* the "Zone"/roadside-picnic specifics.
- **Walter M. Miller Jr.** — *TAKE:* knowledge and memory preserved across long dark ages; faith
  treated seriously; cyclical, humbling history. *AVOID:* the canticle, the order, the names.
- **John Brunner** — *TAKE:* sociological extrapolation — society reasoned about as a system, with the
  people kept human. *AVOID:* his named near-futures and characters.
- **Octavia E. Butler** — *TAKE:* resilient communities; survival and power examined honestly; change
  as the one constant; hard-won hope and radical empathy. *AVOID:* Earthseed, the Patternist worlds,
  character names.
- **Samuel R. Delany · Joanna Russ · James Tiptree Jr.** — *TAKE:* identity, the body, gender, and the
  outsider's view explored with seriousness; bold social imagination. *AVOID:* their specific worlds,
  characters, and coinages.

(Add to this list as you find your own touchstones — keep the same TAKE / AVOID discipline.)

## 4. The core themes that build THIS world

Distil the influences above into the load-bearing themes to keep hitting. Each maps to a cornerstone
file so you know where it lives:

1. **Distance is the central fact.** Weeks between worlds; the dark between is a *place*, not empty
   space. → `05-worlds.md`, `75-technology.md` (the slowness is deliberate and load-bearing).
2. **Connection across distance.** Radio, letters, and the relay are the thread; communication is how
   love and news overcome the void. → `00-station.md`, `78-communication.md`. *(Le Guin, Bradbury.)*
3. **Mature wonder & the sublime.** Awe without despair; a vast indifferent cosmos met with warmth.
   → `80-cosmos.md`. *(Clarke, Stapledon.)*
4. **Optimism tempered by hard questions.** Neither utopia nor dystopia; problems reasoned through.
   → `30-polities.md`, `35-economy.md`, `45-conflict.md`. *(Le Guin, Brunner.)*
5. **The dignity of ordinary life.** The human heart in a huge setting; small things made luminous.
   → `50-daily-life.md`. *(Bradbury, Heinlein.)*
6. **Deep time & memory.** History long, non-linear, humbling; the fossil-word "settlement"; knowledge
   kept across the ages. → `10-history.md`, `55-language.md`. *(Asimov, Miller, Cordwainer Smith.)*
7. **The anthropology of difference.** Peoples shaped by their worlds; otherness taken seriously, never
   caricatured. → `20-peoples.md`. *(Le Guin.)*
8. **Moral seriousness about machines & the unknown.** Thinking machines as ethics puzzles; the alien
   left genuinely unknown; humanity, so far, alone. → `25-other-minds.md`. *(Asimov, Lem.)*
9. **Meaning sought honestly.** Faith and art as serious human universals in an indifferent cosmos.
   → `60-faith.md`, `65-arts.md` (the tribute's truest home), `70-music.md`.

## 5. Tone & voice (the house style)

**Aim for:** mature wonder; cozy, intelligent, a little wry; warm; morally serious; optimism tempered
by hard questions. The DJs speak as if sitting beside **one** listener in a dim room, even though the
broadcast crosses the dark — distance is part of the grammar.

**Steer clear of:** dystopian gloom, grimdark, camp, pulp spectacle, militarism, detached irony, and —
above all — modern-AI anxiety (§2). Wonder is found in small things as much as large.

## 6. How to turn a strain into canon (worked examples)

The move is always the same: **keep the feeling, invent the specifics.**

| Influence (the strain) | → Original canon (no IP) | Lives in |
|---|---|---|
| Asimov's robot-ethics puzzles | "Machine minds are capable and common as tools, but by settled custom they are not held to be persons." | `25-other-minds.md` |
| Le Guin's communication-across-distance | "The relays are the thread that connects scattered worlds; conversations are measured in weeks." | `78-communication.md` |
| Bradbury's elegiac, fondly-remembered Earth | "Earth endures as distant origin, spoken of fondly… half record, half founding myth." | `10-history.md` |
| Miller's knowledge kept through dark ages | *(yours to write)* a faith/arts fact about a people who preserve memory across the long dark. | `60-faith.md` / `65-arts.md` |

Notice what's gone: no "Three Laws", no "ansible", no named work — only the idea, re-dressed.

## 7. The writer's checklist

**Do**
- Channel a *strain*, then invent original names, terms, and details.
- Keep **distance** and **connection** central; aim for **mature wonder**.
- Stay consistent with what's already fixed (`00-station.md`, `01-time.md`, and the rest).
- Follow the format ([`README.md`](README.md)) and tag from the palette ([`TAGS.md`](TAGS.md)).

**Don't**
- Name a real author, work, character, place, species, ship, institution, coined term, or quotable
  line — *anywhere* in canon.
- Reach for modern-AI tropes (§2), dystopia, or camp.
- Hardcode a fixed year (the in-world year is always real year + 600).
- Contradict the established premise.

**Workflow:** read this brief → `README.md` (format) → `TAGS.md` (tags) → write your `## Topic` prose
and `## Canon facts` (with tags) in the cornerstone files → run `make seed-canon` → check the counts
grew and the facts read true.
